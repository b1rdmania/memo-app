[5] Date: 28 April 2026

[6] You instruct us in connection with your dismissal by Acme Trading Ltd ("Acme") on 12 March 2026, after three years and two months of continuous service as a sales executive. Acme cite gross misconduct, specifically the alleged misuse of a corporate credit card on 4 February 2026 in the sum of £1,840.20 at a restaurant in Mayfair. You accept the expenditure but maintain it was a legitimate client entertainment cost incurred on behalf of a prospective customer, BlueLight Logistics Ltd.

[7] You have continuous service of three years and two months and are therefore a qualifying employee under section 108 of the Employment Rights Act 1996. The dismissal falls within the unfair dismissal jurisdiction of the Employment Tribunal under section 94 ERA 1996.

[8] Acme rely on conduct as the potentially fair reason under section 98(2)(b) ERA 1996. The tribunal will apply the well-known test in British Home Stores v Burchell [1980] ICR 303: did the employer hold a genuine belief in the misconduct, on reasonable grounds, after a reasonable investigation. The range of reasonable responses test (Iceland Frozen Foods v Jones [1983] ICR 17) applies to the sanction.

[9] On the procedural side, we are concerned about three points. First, the investigation appears to have been conducted by the same manager who chaired the disciplinary hearing, in apparent breach of the ACAS Code of Practice on Disciplinary and Grievance Procedures. Second, you were not provided with the documents relied upon (the credit card statement and the manager's investigation note) until the hearing itself. Third, you were dismissed at the disciplinary hearing without an adjournment to consider the evidence, and the appeal was heard by the dismissing officer's line manager.

[10] On the merits, your strongest point is that BlueLight Logistics signed a £42,000 contract with Acme on 18 February 2026 — two weeks after the dinner. The link between the entertainment and the resulting business is documented in your handover note to the new account manager. We have a copy.

[11] Acme's strongest point is paragraph 4.7 of the staff handbook, which requires pre-approval for client entertainment over £500. You did not seek pre-approval. Your position is that pre-approval was waived in practice by your line manager, Tom Reeves, who has now left the business. Without Mr Reeves's evidence this will be hard to prove.

[12] The compensatory award is likely to be modest. You secured alternative employment on 5 May 2026 at a salary £6,400 lower than your Acme package. Mitigation is good. Past loss to date is approximately £4,100; future loss to one-year cap approximately £6,400. Basic award (three weeks at the capped weekly figure of £700) is £2,100. Indicative total compensation: £12,600 plus the statutory uplift for ACAS Code breach (up to 25%, so potentially £15,750).

[13] Time limits: the EDT is 12 March 2026. ACAS Early Conciliation Day A was notified on 2 May 2026; Day B is 24 May 2026. The primary three-month limitation period expired on 11 June 2026 but is extended by the "stop the clock" provisions in section 207B ERA 1996 to 3 July 2026.

[14] We are now within two weeks of the extended deadline. ET1 must be filed by 3 July 2026. Recommend you confirm instructions to issue by 24 June 2026 at the latest to allow drafting time.

[15] As to settlement, a without-prejudice approach to Acme's solicitors (Hodgson & Pike) is realistic. Our preliminary view is that a settlement in the range of £8,000 to £12,000 plus a reference would be a reasonable commercial outcome, reflecting the procedural weakness in Acme's case and the litigation risk to both parties.

[16] We strongly advise you do not contact any Acme employee directly, and that all communications go through this firm. The credit card statement and your handover note should be preserved in their original form pending disclosure.

[17] Please let us know by Friday 9 May 2026 whether you wish us to proceed with ACAS conciliation in parallel with preparing the ET1, or whether you would prefer to pursue early settlement first.
